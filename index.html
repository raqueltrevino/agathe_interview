BLOSSOMING ARTIST: AGATHE RENOU

Agathe Renou is an artist currently in her second year at Parsons School Of Design. She is a communication designer who uses art not only as a means of self-expression but also as a tool for creating awareness on the absurdity of societal standards. Although she mostly does this through the use of photography and typography, she enjoys exploring several different artistic styles but often finds herself drawn to surrealism. Art has created her ambition and given her the means to amplify her ideas. She wants to continue developing her creative capacities by working outside her comfort zone, examining things from different angles, and questioning her surroundings.

RT: WHAT'S YOUR AESTHETIC?

AR: i have never had a certain aesthetic, i could never choose just one. i think instead of sticking to one style i branch out a lot more to test the waters, taking inspiration from different styles and trying to make them my own.

RT: WHAT ART DO YOU IDENTIFY MOST WITH?

AR: i find myself rearing to surrealism a lot. it's all about releasing your creativity and expressing your idea of expression. the best part is different people read and understand your art differently, having their own perspective differ from yours.

RT: WHAT THEMES DO YOU LIKE TO EXPLORE IN YOUR ART PRACTICE?

AR: i really love expressing important messages to break society standards, i think it should be spoken more about. i hate society standards and I wish to show more how meaningless they are through my artwork.

RT: IS THAT WHY YOU CHOSE TO STUDY ART AT PARSONS SCHOOL OF DESIGN?

AR: i've always turned to art as a way of escape. i found myself in a rough time and found a way to express myself for the better. when i do it, i feel free.

RT: HOW DO YOU WORK?

AR: when i'm doing art, i'm either frustrated or at peace. when i'm at peace, i have music playing and candles lit, and i find myself working calmly and smoothly. however i get easily frustrated when something comes out differently than i expected, causing me many breakdowns and leaving me unmotivated.

RT: WHAT WORK DO YOU MOST ENJOY DOING?

AR: i love incorporating messages with photography, which is why i've chosen the communications design major. i find myself more enthusiastic when i work digitally and combine words to photos to complete a poster.

RT: WHAT IS YOUR DREAM ART/DESIGN PROJECT?

AR: i would love it if we could make a poster about something political. as art is a huge factor in spreading awareness. i wish we could take advantage of our speciality and use it for the better in talking about major current events going on.

RT: WHAT ROLE DOES ART HAVE IN OUR SOCIETY?

AR: art is used everywhere in our society. art is fashion, posters, architecture, websites- art is everywhere. not only is it a tool of self expression, it's also a tool to bring others in.

RT: WHAT DO YOU DISLIKE MOST ABOUT THE ARTWORLD?

AR: i hate how competitive the art industry is. already, i'm a very competitive person. i always find myself comparing my art with others, leaving me feeling distraught and never good enough. it's hard to push yourself when you feel like you're never good enough. however, at the end of the day, i need to remind myself that i am good enough and that i can do this.

RT: WHAT'S THE BEST PIECE OF ADVICE YOU'VE BEEN GIVEN?

AR: i was always reminded by my art teacher in high school that art is never finished. there is always room for revision and change, meaning there is always more you can do to make your art feel more like you.
